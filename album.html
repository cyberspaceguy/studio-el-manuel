The Studio Review: Gorillaz - 'The Mountain'

By Emmanuel Mutaka | Art Critic

If you’ve been following Gorillaz lately, you know they’ve spent a lot of time in the neon-lit world of synth-pop. But with their ninth studio album, The Mountain, the band has stepped out of the club and into the sunlight. For those of us who live for the pure musicality of a project, this album is a masterpiece of instrumental layering and melodic songwriting.

The track "The Manifestor" is a definitive highlight, captivating with its storytelling that paints vivid pictures. It feels like being immersed in a television show, playing directly through your ears with a cinematic production style that only Gorillaz can pull off.

Additionally, the song "The Happy Dictator" is quite interesting, offering deep reflections through a incredibly melodic lens. It balances its satirical themes with a catchy, modern mood that stays in your head long after the track ends. The album overall provides a real hip-hop experience with a very soulful, organic foundation.

Critique Notes

Vivid Imagery

Lyrical detail in "The Manifestor" makes you a spectator in a grand story rather than just a listener.

Melodic Songwriting

Tracks like "The Happy Dictator" prove that catchy hooks and intellectual themes can live perfectly in the same space.

Instrumental Layering

The use of harmonic instrumentals gives the album a soulful, organic foundation that ties the project together.

Narrative Variety

Every track is a different "episode" with its own unique story and atmosphere, creating a true journey from start to finish.
