The Studio Review: Gorillaz - 'The Mountain'

By Emmanuel Mutaka | Art Critic

If you’ve been following Gorillaz lately, you know they’ve spent a lot of time in the neon-lit world of synth-pop. But with their ninth studio album, The Mountain, the band has stepped out of the club and into a lush, sunlight-filled soundscape. For those of us who live for pure musicality, this album is a masterclass in melodic pop and instrumental layering.

The track "The Manifestor" is a definitive highlight, with an instrumental arrangement so cinematic it feels like a total immersion in sound. The way the melodies swell and transition is breathtaking, showcasing a level of production that only Gorillaz can achieve in the modern pop landscape.

Additionally, "The Happy Dictator" stands out with its incredible melodic hooks. It’s an earworm in the best possible way, balancing complex, organic instrumentals with a catchy, bright mood that feels both fresh and timeless. The entire project is grounded in a soulful, harmonic foundation that makes it an absolute joy to listen to from start to finish.

Critique Notes

Sonic Atmosphere

The arrangement on "The Manifestor" creates a massive, immersive world through intricate sound design and sweeping chords.

Pop Perfection

"The Happy Dictator" highlights the band's ability to craft infectious, radio-ready hooks without losing musical depth.

Instrumental Layering

A focus on harmonic, live-feeling instruments gives the album an organic foundation that ties the global pop sounds together.

Melodic Complexity

The album prioritizes rich, soaring melodies over simple loops, rewarding the listener with something new to hear on every playback.
